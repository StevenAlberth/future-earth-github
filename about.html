How We Shape the Future

PRIMARY VALUE

Science-Based

Our content is built on reviewed studies and climate datasets from established institutions. We prioritize sources with clear methodology, transparent publication records, and consistent citation standards.

  • References are crosschecked across multiple sources.
  • Key claims are reviewed before publication.
  • Updates are aligned with major report releases.

Credibility · Accuracy · Research
Access · Clarity · Reach

Inclusive

We design explanations for mixed audiences, from first time readers to learners who want deeper context.

  • Clear language with practical examples.
  • Balanced framing across local and global impact.

Implementation · Responsibility · Impact

Action-Oriented

We pair each climate issue with practical actions that are realistic for individuals, schools, and communities.

  • Focus on measurable steps over broad slogans.
  • Prioritize actions linked to evidence based outcomes.

Partnership · Dialogue · Shared Learning

Collaborative

We treat climate progress as shared work across students, communities, institutions, and policy networks.

  • Built to support discussion, not one way messaging.
  • Encourages coordinated action across different roles.

HISTORY

Key Climate Milestones

Drag or use arrows to travel through time →

Climate Science · 01 / 06
1988
IPCC Established
The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) was established to provide governments around the world with reliable, science-based assessments on climate change, its impacts, and the actions needed to address it.
International Policy · 02 / 06
1997
Kyoto Protocol
The first binding international agreement that committed industrialized nations to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, marking a turning point in global climate action.
Public Awareness · 03 / 06
2006
An Inconvenient Truth
Al Gore's documentary brought the climate crisis into living rooms worldwide, inspiring a new generation of environmental advocates and accelerating global discourse.
Global Agreement · 04 / 06
2015
Paris Agreement
196 countries came together to limit global temperature rise to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels, marking one of the most ambitious climate commitments ever made.
Scientific Warning · 05 / 06
2018
IPCC 1.5°C Report
Scientists warned that keeping global warming to 1.5°C would demand rapid, unprecedented changes across energy, land use, industry, and society within just over a decade.
Record Breaking · 06 / 06
2023
Hottest Year on Record
It was confirmed as the hottest year in recorded history, with global average temperatures rising to around 1.45°C above pre-industrial levels and breaking every previous record.
