FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Future Earth project? ▼
Future Earth is a student-created interactive web project focused on raising awareness about climate change and environmental issues. We use data visualization, interactive tools, and educational content to help people understand the urgency of the climate crisis and what they can do about it.
Where does your data come from? ▼
All our data is sourced from internationally recognized scientific organizations including NASA, the IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change), NOAA, UNEP, WWF, and peer-reviewed scientific journals such as Nature and Science. We strive to keep our information current and accurate.
Why did you create this project? ▼
We created Future Earth to raise awareness about the urgency of climate change and to show that everyone has a role in shaping the future. Small actions, when multiplied, can lead to meaningful change.
Can this site be used for educational purposes? ▼
Yes, Our content is created for educational purposes and we encourage sharing it for non-commercial use. If you use our materials, please credit Future Earth and link back to our website.
How can I take action on climate change? ▼
Visit our Content page and explore the “Take Action” section to discover simple yet impactful ways you can help the planet. From reducing plastic use to making more sustainable lifestyle choices, every step matters. You can also try our interactive waste-sorting minigame to test your knowledge and take part in our pledge to commit to real change. Every action, no matter how small, makes a difference.
